Hi! I'm a third-year PhD student in the Computational and Data-Enabled Sciences at The State University of New York at Buffalo, advised by Professor Kaiyi Ji. My research focuses on Lifelong Learning, Transfer learning for large language models (LLMs), NLP, Machine Learning and Deep Learning.

These days, I’m working on prompt-based continual learning, enabling language models to acquire new tasks incrementally through lightweight prompt updates instead of full retraining. The goal is to retain prior knowledge, adapt rapidly, and prevent unbounded prompt growth.
In my recent work, I introduced GRID, which uses gradient signals to decide which prompts to reuse, refine, or compress, and pairs this with task-aware decoding so the model consistently selects the right knowledge at inference. Feel free to check out the project!

I’m further exploring frameworks that go beyond forgetting, enabling positive backward transfer, where learning new tasks can even improve performance on earlier ones, by refining previously learned knowledge so it not only remains stable but also benefits from new learning.

I am actively looking for summer 2026 internships in areas related to AI/ML. If my research interests align with your team, please don't hesitate to reach out!
